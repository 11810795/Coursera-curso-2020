To be bounded, there should be some bound. Let's call it \(M\).

\(M\) is a real number. We can write that as \(M \in \mathbb{R}\).

We are considering a sequence \(a_n\).

To be bounded below, every single term in the sequence must be bigger than \(M\).

To be bounded below, for every single term \(a_n\) in the sequence, \(a_n > M\).

To be bounded below, for all \(n \in \mathbb{N}\), we have \(a_n > M\).

Putting it all together, "bounded below" means that there exists an , so that for all \(n \in \mathbb{N}\), we have \(a_n > M\).
