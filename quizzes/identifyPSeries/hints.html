Note that \(\displaystyle\sum_{#{variable}=#{initial}}^\infty \left( #{latex(term)} \right)\) converges iff \(\sum_{#{variable}=#{initial}} \frac{1}{#{variable}^{#{latex(p)}}}\) converges.

Note that \(\sum_{#{variable}=#{initial}} \frac{1}{#{variable}^{#{latex(p)}}}\) is a \(p\)-series.

But \(\sum_{#{variable}=#{initial}} \frac{1}{#{variable}^{#{latex(p)}}}\) is the tail of a \(p\)-series.

In this case, \(p = #{latex(p)}\).

Since \(p = #{latex(p)} \leq 1\), we may conclude that the given series diverges.

Since \(p = #{latex(p)} > 1\), we may conclude that the given series converges.
